Ace Your Resume: Essential Tips

How To Write a Strong Professional Summary?


A resume summary, also known as a professional summary or summary statement, is a short description at the top of your resume that describes your experience, qualities and skills. Your professional summary is your first impression. Make it count!


  • Length: Keep it concise—2-3 sentences long.
  • Customization: Tailor it for each job application using keywords from the job description.
  • Professional Background: Include your job title, years of experience, and relevant industries.
  • Core Skills: Mention both hard and soft skills pertinent to the role in short.
  • Value Proposition: Explain how you can contribute to the employer’s success.

Example: For a Recent Graduate

Motivated Computer Science graduate with hands-on software development experience through internships. Proficient in Python and Java, with a solid foundation in data structures and algorithms. Demonstrated strong analytical and problem-solving skills through academic projects, including a web application that enhanced user engagement by 30%. Eager to apply technical skills and passion for technology to contribute to a dynamic tech team while continuing to grow professionally.

Education Section


Your education background is important, especially if you are a recent graduate.


  • Order: List your education in reverse chronological order (most recent first).
  • Degree: Specify your degree and major (if applicable).
  • Institution: Name the school, college, or university.
  • Graduation Date: Include month and year.
  • GPA/Percentage: Include if it's impressive.

Highlighting Skills Effectively


Make sure to showcase the skills that set you apart.


  • Relevance: Tailor skills to the job description.
  • Categorization: Categorize the skills into hard skills (field related skills) as well as soft skills (interpersonal skills).
  • Be honest: Do not list skills you don't possess.
  • Avoid Overloading: Limit to 8-12 relevant skills.

Detailing Work Experience


Your work experience section should showcase your career journey.


  • Reverse Chronological Order: List jobs starting with the most recent position.
  • Include Key Information: Job Title, Company Name, Location, Dates of Employment.
  • Quantify Achievements: Whenever possible, include numbers or metrics to showcase your accomplishments (e.g., “Increased sales by 20%”).
  • Projects: If applicable, mention significant projects and your role in them.

Achievements and awards section


Mentioning achievements on your resume demonstrates your skills, value, and contributions, helping you stand out to potential employers.


  • Relevance: Include achievements that are relevant to the job you’re applying for. This could include academic honors, professional awards, or notable projects.
  • Specificity: Be specific about each achievement. Include the name of the award or recognition, the organization that granted it, and the date received.
  • Quantify When Possible: Use numbers to illustrate the impact of your achievements (e.g., “Awarded Employee of the Month for increasing sales by 25%”).

Additional Tips


The way your resume looks can affect its readability.


  • Use a clean and professional layout.
  • Keep your font size between 10-12 points.
  • Use consistent formatting for headings and bullet points.
  • Leave adequate white space for easy reading.
  • Avoid using jargons.
  • Double-check for typos, grammar mistakes, and inaccuracies.
